MANHATTAN LANDLORDS HAVE TROUBLE FINDING TENANTS

A study conducted by StreetEasy, New York City’s leading local real estate marketplace, found that for the first time in a decade the average rent in Manhattan declined. It fell from 3,000 to 2,990. According to experts in the industry, this is a historic low that illustrates the impact of the same pandemic that caused many residents to leave the city. The rent index fell for each borough. Manhattan’s fell to 7.8 percent, in Brooklyn it is 2.5 percent, and in the lowest in Queens with 2.2 percent. In addition, StreetEast notes the inventory of apartments has increased by 69.8 percent in Manhattan. There were 72,267 listings available in July, August, and September— the months following the reopening of NYC.
